# No-Relicense Pledge

_Dated 2026-06-16._

relyable is released under the Apache License 2.0. This document is a plain,
public commitment about what that means and what it will keep meaning.

## The pledge

1. **Released code stays Apache-2.0.** Every version of relyable we have
   published under Apache-2.0 is, and will remain, Apache-2.0. We will not
   retroactively relicense a released version, withdraw it, or move it behind
   a more restrictive license.

2. **The re-derivation engine stays open.** The gate, the verdict/skill/memory
   surfaces over it, and the Hermes and OpenClaw adapters are the open
   substrate. We will not move the engine to a closed or source-available
   license to drive upgrades.

3. **The open tool is not a funnel.** relyable does real work standalone, with
   no account, no paid dependency, and no network call to reach a verdict. We
   will not cripple it to sell something else, and we will not add a feature
   only to gate it.

4. **No silent telemetry, ever.** relyable does not phone home. If any
   telemetry is ever added, it will be opt-in, off by default, and documented
   exactly.

5. **No copyright-assignment CLA.** Contributions are accepted under the
   Developer Certificate of Origin (see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)). We
   do not ask contributors to assign copyright — which is, deliberately, the
   legal precondition for the relicense we are pledging never to do.

## What we do sell — stated plainly

relyable rides the open [veriker](https://github.com/veriker/veriker)
re-derivation substrate, and both relyable and veriker are and stay Apache-2.0.
We also build NEXIVERIFY, a commercial audit-trail product for regulated teams.
It monetizes a *layer around* verification — hosting, retention, trusted-time
and cross-organization attestation, conformance evidence, and support. It never
monetizes the verdict itself: the open gate always produces the same answer,
offline, for free. If that ever stops being true, this pledge has been broken,
and you should hold us to it.
